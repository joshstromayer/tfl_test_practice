    Exam Strategy (Quick Wins)

    • Slow down on punctuation: full stops, commas after long openers, apostrophes for possession (customer’s card / customers’ cards).
    • Read aloud in your head: if you run out of breath, it’s probably a run-on sentence—split it.
    • Capital letters: start of sentences, days, months, job titles when used before a name (e.g., Station Manager Khan).
    • Agreement: singular subject → singular verb (the train arrives), plural subject → plural verb (the trains arrive).
    • Homophones: its/it’s, your/you’re, there/their/they’re. If you can say “it is”, use it’s.
    • Numbers & time: be consistent (09:05 not 9.05); write clearly.

    Aim for accuracy over speed. One perfect paragraph beats three rushed ones.
